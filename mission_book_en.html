🟣 Science Note · Aerodynamics Wind = moving air! Warm air rises and cool air rushes in — that’s wind. Strong wind can even make electricity (wind power)!

🟣 Art Note · Impasto Van Gogh used very thick paint! Up close the painting is bumpy; from far away the sky looks like it’s really moving!

DAY 8 · Korea Special · Culture + Creativity

The Dancing Mask
(Talchum 탈춤)

Moong found an old wooden mask in a Korean village. When the drums began to play — dung, dung! — the mask started to smile… and dance! “Every Korean mask has its own face and feeling. What would YOUR mask feel?”

🟢 Did you know? · Korea
  • Korean masks are called Tal (탈). The famous Hahoe masks are over 800 years old!
  • Talchum (탈춤) — the Korean mask dance — is on the UNESCO list. Dancers wear masks to tell funny, brave, and kind stories.
  • The Korean alphabet, Hangeul (한글), was created so that everyone could read and write.
  • Hanbok (한복) is the colorful traditional Korean dress.
🟣 Culture Note · Feelings in motion In talchum, each mask shows one big feeling — a wide laugh, a sly grin, a gentle smile. The dance turns feelings into movement (just like Mongle creatures coming to life!).
